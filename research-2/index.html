Overall, the data shows trends over the years, how in pre-industrial society, there was a trend decrease of CO2 levels. This changed during industrialization, reinforcing that atmospheric CO2 levels have been on the rise since 1850 A.D.. Having access to other long term trend of CO2 allows for constructing a more informed prediction of the future.

Source 2: Deforestation and Forest Degradation: The Causes, Effects, and Solutions

This article This article covers the basics of deforestation vs. degradation, both are problems. This is highlighting how ecosystems are being hurt by human activity. This source gives an overall view deforestation, and explains the global significance of forest ecosystems. It explains that the leading causes of deforestation are logging industry, agriculture, wildfires, mining, and infrastructure. These overall will have consequences stemming from deforestation. It leads to a diminished ability for the Earth to capture and store greenhouse gases. It also leads to a loss of biodiversity in forest ecosystems. Deforestation can lead to other climate consequences such as flooding, soil erosion, as well as affecting other ecosystems and communities directly. Another consequence is changing climate patterns. The source gives an overall view but also provides some solutions, it creates a path forward by being speculative.
